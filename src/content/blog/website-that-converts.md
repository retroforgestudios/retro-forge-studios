When someone lands on your website, you have about five seconds before they
decide to stay or hit the back button. A pretty site that fails that test still
loses the customer. Here are the five things a homepage needs to actually convert
a visitor into a lead.

## 1. A headline that says what you do and who it's for

The number one homepage mistake is being vague or clever instead of clear. In one
glance a visitor should know exactly what you offer and whether it's for them.
"Same-day appliance repair in Omaha" beats "Excellence you can trust" every time.

## 2. One obvious next step

Every page should have a single, clear call-to-action repeated where the eye
lands — *Call now*, *Book a free estimate*, *Get a quote*. If visitors have to
hunt for how to contact you, most won't.

## 3. Proof you're legit

People buy from businesses they trust. Put your trust signals up front: real
reviews, recognizable logos or certifications, real photos of your work and your
team. Borrowed credibility beats adjectives.

## 4. It has to work on a phone

The majority of your visitors are on mobile. If they have to pinch, zoom, or wait
for a slow page, they're gone. A site that isn't fast and effortless on a phone
is leaking customers daily — and you'd never see it in your analytics as anything
but a "bounce."

## 5. Speed

Every extra second of load time costs you visitors. Big images, bloated
plugins, and cheap hosting quietly kill conversions. Fast is a feature.

## The honest part

Notice that none of these are about looking fancy — they're about being *clear,
fast, and pointed at one action*. That's the difference between a website that
sits there and one that brings in work.

For a local business, reviews are everything. They're the first thing customers
check, and they directly affect whether you show up in Google's local results.
The good news: getting more of them isn't about luck or nagging — it's about
having a simple, repeatable ask. Here's how.

## Ask at the peak moment

The best time to ask is right when the customer is happiest — the job's done,
they're thrilled, the value is fresh. Wait a week and that feeling fades. Build
the ask into the moment you finish.

## Make it stupidly easy

Every extra step loses people. Don't say "look us up on Google." Instead:

- Use your **Google review short link** (Google gives you one) and text or email it directly.
- Put a **QR code** on the invoice, the counter, the receipt.
- One tap, leave a review, done.

## Just ask — out loud

The single biggest reason businesses don't get reviews is that they never
actually ask. Most happy customers are glad to help; they just don't think of it.
A simple "If you've got 30 seconds, a quick Google review really helps us out"
works wonders.

## Respond to every one

Thank the good ones. Respond calmly and professionally to the bad ones (future
customers read how you handle problems). Google also favors businesses that
engage with their reviews.

## Never buy fake reviews

It's against Google's rules, customers can smell it, and it can get your profile
suspended. A steady trickle of real reviews beats a pile of fake ones every time.

## The honest part

The hard part isn't any single step — it's doing it *every time*, consistently,
until it's a habit baked into how you operate.

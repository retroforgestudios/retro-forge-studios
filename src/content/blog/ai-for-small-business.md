Every other ad right now promises AI will run your whole marketing for you. As
someone who uses these tools every single day, let me give you the honest
version: AI is an incredible *accelerator* and a terrible *autopilot*.

Here's how to tell the difference.

## Where AI genuinely helps

- **First drafts.** Staring at a blank page is the slowest part of writing. AI gets you to a rough draft in seconds so you can spend your time improving instead of starting.
- **Ideas and angles.** Stuck on what to post? AI is great for brainstorming directions you can pick from.
- **Research and summarizing.** Competitor scans, keyword ideas, turning a long transcript into notes — huge time savers.
- **Repurposing.** Turning one blog post into five social captions, fast.

## Where AI hurts you

- **Publishing it raw.** Unedited AI content is generic, slightly off, and sounds like everyone else's unedited AI content. Customers can feel it.
- **Facts and specifics.** AI will confidently state things that are wrong. Anything with numbers, names, or claims needs a human check.
- **Your actual voice.** AI doesn't know your story, your town, or the way you talk to customers. That's the part that earns trust — and it can't be faked.

## The approach that actually works

Use AI for speed, keep a human in charge of judgment. Draft fast, then a real
person edits for accuracy, voice, and whether it's actually *good*. That's not a
compromise — it's the best of both: the output of a big team at the speed of one,
without sounding like a robot.

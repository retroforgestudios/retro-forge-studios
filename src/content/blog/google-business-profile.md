If you only do one marketing thing this month, make it this: fix your Google
Business Profile. It's free, it's the first thing customers see when they search
for you, and it's the single biggest lever for showing up in local results — yet
most businesses claim it, fill in three fields, and never touch it again.

Here's how to actually use it.

## Fill out *everything*

Google rewards complete profiles. The businesses that show up in the map "3-pack"
almost always have every field filled in:

- **Categories** — pick the most specific primary category, then add relevant secondary ones.
- **Services & descriptions** — list what you actually do, in plain language customers search for.
- **Hours** — including holiday hours (nothing kills trust like driving to a "closed" shop that said it was open).
- **Service area** — if you go to customers, set the towns you cover.

## Add real photos — and keep adding them

Profiles with photos get dramatically more clicks and direction requests. Use
real photos of your work, your team, your storefront — not stock. Add a few new
ones every month; Google notices activity.

## Post to it like a mini social feed

Most owners don't realize you can post updates, offers, and news right on your
profile. It's free visibility on the exact page where buying decisions happen.
Even a couple of posts a month signals you're active and open.

## Use the Q&A and reviews

- **Q&A:** seed it with the questions you get asked most — and answer them yourself.
- **Reviews:** respond to every single one. It tells both the customer *and Google* you're engaged.

## The honest part

None of this is hard. The catch is that it's never "done" — a Google Business
Profile rewards consistent attention, and that's the thing that slips when you're
busy running the actual business.
